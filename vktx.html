Company Overview

Vikings Therapeutics (VKTX) is a San Diego-based clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company focused on metabolic and endocrine diseases. Founded in 2012 and publicly listed on Nasdaq, the company has built its pipeline around next-generation receptor agonists designed to improve on the efficacy, tolerability, and convenience of existing therapies in obesity and related conditions.

The company's lead program, VK2735, is a dual GLP-1/GIP receptor agonist — the same mechanism underlying the blockbuster weight-loss drugs Ozempic and Mounjaro. Vikings' subcutaneous formulation is in Phase 3 development for obesity, while an oral version of VK2735 is in Phase 2 — a potentially significant differentiator given that oral GLP-1 options remain limited and highly sought after by patients and payers alike. If the oral formulation delivers competitive weight-loss results, it could open a massive market currently dominated by Novo Nordisk and Eli Lilly.

Beyond obesity, VKTX is developing VK2809, a thyroid hormone receptor beta agonist for non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (NASH) and hypercholesterolemia, and VK0612, a fructose-1,6- bisphosphatase inhibitor for type 2 diabetes. These programs provide pipeline depth but are secondary to the VK2735 obesity story in terms of near-term investor focus.

Investor thesis: VKTX is a pure-play bet on the oral GLP-1 opportunity. The obesity drug market is projected to exceed $100 billion annually by 2030, and any company that can deliver an effective oral formulation with a clean side-effect profile stands to capture significant market share. VKTX trades at a fraction of the market cap of Novo Nordisk and Lilly, offering asymmetric upside if Phase 3 data validates its approach. The key risks are clinical — late-stage failures in this class are not uncommon — and commercial, as partnering or building a sales force to compete with established giants will require significant capital or a licensing deal.
