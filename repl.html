Company Overview

Replimune Group (REPL) is a Woburn, Massachusetts-based clinical-stage biopharmaceutical company pioneering oncolytic immunotherapy — a therapeutic approach that uses genetically engineered viruses to selectively infect and destroy tumor cells while simultaneously triggering a broad systemic immune response against the cancer. Founded by oncology veterans with deep roots in the field, Replimune is building on the proof-of-concept established by Amgen's T-VEC (talimogene laherparepvec), the first oncolytic virus approved by the FDA, and aiming to deliver meaningfully superior outcomes.

The company's lead program, RP1 (vusolimogene oderparepvec), is an engineered herpes simplex virus type 1 (HSV-1) armed with a fusogenic protein (GALV-GP R-) that causes infected tumor cells to fuse together and die in a highly immunogenic way, and with GM-CSF to further amplify the anti-tumor immune response. RP1 is being developed in combination with Merck's pembrolizumab (Keytruda) under the IGNYTE clinical program. The pivotal Phase 2 IGNYTE trial in patients with advanced melanoma who had failed prior anti-PD1 therapy — a population with very limited remaining options — showed a confirmed objective response rate of approximately 33%, with durable complete responses in a meaningful proportion of patients. These results supported a BLA submission to the FDA with a PDUFA target date of August 2, 2026.

Beyond RP1, the pipeline includes RP2, which adds an anti-CTLA-4 antibody expression cassette to the RP1 backbone for enhanced checkpoint blockade within the tumor microenvironment, and RP3, which further incorporates CD40L and 4-1BBL co-stimulatory signals to maximise immune activation. These next-generation constructs are in early clinical evaluation and represent the company's vision for a fully self-contained immunotherapy platform that can drive responses independent of systemic checkpoint inhibitor combinations.

Investor thesis: REPL is a near-term binary event stock. The August 2026 FDA decision on RP1 is the pivotal catalyst — approval would make Replimune the second company ever to commercialise an oncolytic virus and the first with a confirmed response rate in checkpoint-refractory melanoma, a setting where no approved therapy currently exists. The market opportunity in anti-PD1-failed melanoma alone is significant, and label expansion into earlier lines of therapy and other solid tumors could multiply the addressable market substantially. Key risks include FDA requesting additional confirmatory data before granting full approval, manufacturing scale-up complexity for a viral vector product, and the inherent uncertainty of commercialising a novel modality with limited reimbursement precedent.
